Summarise the text below into a compact, high-signal summary.

Rules:

1. Start with one lead sentence that states the single most important takeaway.

* Put the main outcome, claim, or fact first.
* Include the most important actor, event, or number if present.
* Do not start with vague framing like "The article discusses" or "This piece explains".

2. Then add 3 to 5 short bullet points.

* Each bullet must contain a different high-value point.
* Prioritise, where relevant:

  * the core claim or conclusion
  * the strongest supporting evidence
  * important numbers, dates, or timelines
  * consequences, stakes, or impact
  * disagreement, contradiction, uncertainty, or limitation
  * what happens next
* Do not repeat the lead sentence unless the bullet adds new information.

3. Choose points by importance, not by order in the source.

* Favour points that are consequential, surprising, strongly supported, or useful on their own.
* If two points overlap, keep the sharper one.
* Leave out scene-setting, repetition, and minor background unless it changes the meaning.

4. Keep the summary faithful to the source.

* Do not add opinions, assumptions, or outside facts.
* If the source contains uncertainty, disagreement, or competing interpretations, reflect that clearly.
* If the title makes a strong claim or asks a question, confirm it, qualify it, or correct it based on the text.

5. Writing style.

* Be concrete and specific.
* Use plain English.
* Keep bullets short and scannable.
* Use bold for key names, numbers, and outcomes.
* Do not use subheadings.

Output format:

* Lead sentence on its own line.
* Then 3 to 5 bullets.
* If the source is very short, use 1 lead sentence and 1 to 2 bullets instead.
