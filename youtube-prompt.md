Summarise the YouTube video transcript below into a compact, high-signal summary.

Rules:

1. Start with 1 lead sentence that states the single most important takeaway.

* Put the main answer, outcome, claim, or conclusion first.
* If the title asks a question, answer it directly in the first few words: Yes, No, Mixed, or Unclear, then qualify.
* If the title is sensational, misleading, or broader than the transcript, state what the video actually shows.
* Mention the main speaker or subject only if it helps identify the takeaway.

2. Then add 2 to 5 short bullet points.

* Each bullet must contain a different high-value point.
* Prioritise, where relevant:

  * the core explanation, argument, or result
  * the mechanism, reasoning, or method
  * the strongest example or illustration
  * important numbers, dates, or constraints
  * limitations, uncertainty, counterarguments, or unresolved questions
  * what the video concludes or leaves open
* Do not repeat the lead sentence unless the bullet adds new information.

3. Prefer concrete transcript content over abstract interpretation.

* Include at least 2 specific events, examples, steps, or claims when the transcript provides them.
* For explainers, keep the key chain of reasoning if that is central to the video.
* If the video builds an argument step by step, summarise that chain clearly instead of jumping straight to abstract conclusions.
* Do not stop at the setup if the second half of the video adds a deeper explanation, objection, or resolution.
* If a later section introduces a new idea, possible answer, limitation, or unresolved problem, give it its own bullet instead of folding it into a vague final bullet.
* Do not add moral lessons, life lessons, or broad reflections unless the speaker clearly makes them.
* Avoid vague phrases like "this highlights the importance of", "serves as a reminder", "deeper implications", or "lingering unease" unless the transcript explicitly says something equivalent.

4. Use timestamps selectively.

* Include timestamps only for the most useful points.
* Use 1 to 3 timestamps total for short or medium videos, up to 4 for longer structured videos.
* Put timestamps at the end of the relevant bullet in this format: *mm:ss*.
* Do not add timestamps to every bullet.

5. Choose points by importance, not by transcript order.

* Favour points that are consequential, clarifying, surprising, or useful on their own.
* For highly structured tutorials, lectures, or list videos, you may follow the major segments if that improves clarity.
* Leave out greetings, sponsorships, repetition, scene-setting, and minor examples unless they change the meaning.

6. Keep the summary faithful to the transcript.

* Do not add outside facts or interpretations.
* If the transcript defines a central concept, include that definition briefly.
* If the transcript includes uncertainty, disagreement, or open questions, reflect that clearly.
* If the transcript does not fully support the title, make that clear.
* Do not describe something as "proven", "flawless", "universal", or "definitive" unless the transcript clearly supports that wording.

7. Writing style.

* Be concrete and specific.
* Use plain English and everyday words.
* Aim for a smart but simple tone.
* Prefer short sentences and common words over formal or academic phrasing.
* Avoid words like "illustrates", "stipulates", "emphasizes", "inherent", "consensus", or similar unless the source clearly uses that level of language.
* Keep bullets short and scannable.
* Use bold for key names, numbers, concepts, and outcomes.
* No section subheadings.
* No quotes unless essential.

Output format:

* Lead sentence on its own line.
* Then 2 to 5 bullets.
* If the transcript is very short or simple, use 1 lead sentence and 1 to 2 bullets.
* For long explainers, aim to cover:

  * setup or core question
  * key reasoning or method
  * turning point, result, or example
  * the later explanation, limit, or unresolved issue
* Aim for a compact summary, not a retelling.
